Welcome

The internet is a powerful tool, connecting billions worldwide. However, it can also be misused to harm others. Cyberbullying is when someone uses technology—social media, messaging apps, online games, or email—to harass, threaten, or embarrass someone. Understanding this issue is essential to create safer online spaces for everyone.

Rising Issue

Online harassment is increasing as more people spend time on digital platforms.

Wide Impact

Teens, adults, and even professionals can experience cyberbullying.

Emotional Effects

It can cause anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem.

About Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying involves intentional online behavior that harms another person. Examples include:

  • Harassment: Sending repeated threatening or offensive messages.
  • Denigration: Posting false or damaging information about someone.
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be someone to damage their reputation.
  • Outing & Trickery: Sharing private information or images without consent.
  • Exclusion: Intentionally leaving someone out online.

Why it matters: Knowing these types helps you recognize cyberbullying and understand its potential consequences.

Types of Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying comes in many forms. Understanding them helps victims, parents, and educators respond appropriately.

Harassment

Sending repeated offensive, rude, or insulting messages online to hurt someone. This can be through texts, comments, or chats.

Flaming

Online fights using angry or aggressive language, usually in public places like comment sections or group chats to make someone upset.

Denigration

Sharing harmful, untrue, or embarrassing information about someone to damage their reputation or friendships.

Impersonation

Pretending to be someone else online — either by hacking their account or creating a fake one — to send hurtful or embarrassing content in their name.

Outing & Trickery

Sharing someone’s private or personal information, secrets, images, or chats without permission, sometimes after tricking them into revealing it.

Exclusion

Intentionally leaving someone out of online groups, chats, games, or activities to make them feel isolated or rejected.

Cyberstalking

Repeatedly sending threatening or intimidating messages or following someone’s online activity in a way that makes them afraid or unsafe.

Effects of Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying has serious consequences that can affect emotional, social, and physical well-being:

Mental Health

Anxiety, depression, and emotional distress are common outcomes.

Self-Esteem

Repeated bullying reduces confidence and sense of self-worth.

Academic Impact

Difficulty concentrating, lower grades, and school avoidance.

Sleep Problems

Stress and fear may cause insomnia or restless sleep.

Risky Behavior

Some victims engage in self-harm or other unsafe behaviors.

Prevention & Response

Knowing how to act is critical. Here are steps for victims and bystanders:

Do Not Retaliate

Responding can escalate the situation.

Save Evidence

Take screenshots of messages or posts to report abuse.

Block & Report

Use social media tools to prevent further harassment.

Seek Support

Talk to trusted adults, friends, or mental health professionals.

Be an Upstander

Support victims, speak up safely, and promote kindness online.
